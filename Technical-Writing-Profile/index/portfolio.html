Portfolio Artifacts

Grant Proposal

Another UMN student and I wrote a mock grant proposal that replicated one that the Center for Victims of Torture would create. The mock project was supported with the following statements: need and problem, goals and objectives, inclusion and diversity, and project sustainability. This proposal was successful because of our special attention to audience analysis.

Formal Report

In this formal report, the language and visuals of Children's Hospital patient education materials were analyzed. Primary and secondary research was conducted to thoroughly analyze whether the technical writing elements were constructive. Recommendations were provided based on this research.

Technical Description

Parts of a Stethoscope is an informative article that describes the components of a stethoscope. Unpracticed medical students may be unaware of each compenent and how it contributes to the stethescope's function. Written instructions allow the medical students to revisit important terminology when using a stethoscope.

Infographic

Adobe InDesign and graphic design principles were used to appeal to UMN students that are studying or interested in biology. Public health communication was also displayed in this infographic to inspire curiosity among students. Plain language was utilized to explain complex biological concepts to a wide range of students.

Instructive Article

This instructive article was written to inform University of Minnesota (UMN) users on how to troubleshoot some of the most common Microsoft Office 365 errors. Audience analysis, peer review, and Greenshot image editing were essential in the drafting process. This article was edited according to the UMN Office of Information Technology style requirements.
